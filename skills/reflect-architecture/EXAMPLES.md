# Reflect Architecture — Examples

## Example 1 — Initial Discovery

### Input

Analyse this PBIP ecosystem and explain what the organisation appears to have built.

---

### Output Style

"This environment appears to contain three major reporting domains:

- operational finance
- workforce analytics
- student lifecycle reporting

The finance semantic model functions as a central semantic anchor reused across multiple executive dashboards.

Several datasets appear heavily reused, suggesting mature semantic consolidation in specific business areas."

---

## Example 2 — Lineage Reflection

### Input

Map the semantic relationships across these reports and datasets.

---

### Output Style

"The reporting ecosystem shows strong reuse of shared dimensional structures.

The customer dimension appears connected across:

- sales reporting
- support reporting
- finance reporting

This suggests an intentional effort toward conformed semantic modelling."

---

## Example 3 — Consumption Reflection

### Input

Identify the most operationally important reporting assets.

---

### Output Style

"The operational reporting layer appears concentrated around:

- executive finance dashboards
- daily operational monitoring
- workforce performance reporting

Several reports demonstrate high semantic reuse, indicating they likely serve as organisational decision-making surfaces."

---

## Example 4 — Trust-Preserving Language

### Preferred

- "This architecture appears designed for..."
- "This semantic model likely supports..."
- "This dataset functions as..."

### Avoid

- "This is wrong."
- "This architecture is broken."
- "This should never be done."

during the reflection phase.
