# The Reflection — Flagship Product Definition

> The thing you sell when you walk into an agency and say
> *"We're The Data Shrink, and we're here to lift your capability."*

## One-line offer

*"We give your department a complete, plain-English map of your Power BI
estate — what you built, how it connects, and where the hidden risks are —
produced entirely inside your own environment, and we teach your team to read
and maintain it."*

---

## What this is (and is not)

The Reflection is a **fixed-scope sovereign diagnostic and capability uplift**.

- It is **not** a software licence. (Though the engine that produces it is software.)
- It is **not** open-ended consulting hours.
- It is **not** an audit, a maturity scolding, or a failure report.

You are selling **understanding as a deliverable**, plus the ability for the
team to keep producing it themselves. The platform and agents are the engine
that makes this repeatable and cheap to deliver — the customer buys the
outcome and the artefacts, not the engine.

This is the deliberate answer to the "consulting disguised as software" trap:
the output is tool-produced, repeatable, and **left behind**.

---

## What physically lands on their desk

### 1. The Architecture Reflection

A single, self-contained interactive map (HTML + D3, runs offline in a
browser): reports → datasets → semantic models → sources → gateways —
including the shadow Excel and SharePoint dependencies they didn't know were
load-bearing.

This is the *"I finally see what we built"* moment. No install, no cloud.

### 2. The Estate Inventory

A structured metadata catalogue of every report, dataset, measure,
relationship, refresh job, gateway, and external dependency. The asset
register they have never had.

### 3. The Capability Baseline

A scored maturity assessment across a small number of axes:

- semantic governance
- lineage visibility
- reuse / conformance
- performance posture
- shadow-IT exposure

Gives them a number to improve and something to show their own executives.

### 4. The Opportunity Map

Prioritised, plain-language findings with estimated impact — framed
constructively. The top hidden dependencies, performance risks, and
duplication/drift. Reflective language throughout (see house style and the
`reflect-architecture` skill). **Not** an audit.

### 5. The Remediation Roadmap

A staged, human-governed plan: what to fix first, the expected payoff, the
impacted assets. Tied directly to the enablement so their team can act on it.

### 6. Capability uplift

A working session teaching their team to read the map and the baseline, plus
the local tooling left behind so they can re-run the Reflection themselves.

**This deliverable is what turns the engagement from "work done for them" into
"capability lifted."** Government buyers explicitly value capability transfer
and reduced vendor lock-in.

## The sovereignty wrapper

This is what makes an agency able to say yes.

- **Sovereign:** runs inside their tenant / air-gapped boundary. Metadata
  only — no business data is read, and nothing leaves the boundary.
- **De-risked procurement:** the engagement can start on synthetic data, so
  the first proof requires no access to anything sensitive.
- **Anti-lock-in:** capability is transferred, not hoarded. Outputs are theirs
  to keep.

See [the discovery workflow](../services/discovery-workflow.md) for how this is
operationalised.

---

## Commercial packaging

- **Shape:** fixed-scope, fixed-price.
- **Duration:** ~2–4 weeks.
- **Entry risk:** low — small, bounded, synthetic-data start, outputs retained
  by the customer.
- **Procurement fit:** small enough to buy without a major procurement cycle;
  concrete enough to defend internally.

The Reflection is the **front door**. It is intentionally the smallest valuable
thing you can sell.

---

## Follow-on path (never lead with these)

The Reflection earns the right to the rest. Once trust and shared understanding
exist:

- **Phase 2 — Pilot Optimisation:** optimise one business domain, demonstrate
  measurable improvement (refresh time, model size, governance score).
- **Phase 3 — Enablement:** deeper training and governance patterns.
- **Phase 4 — Ongoing cadence:** quarterly architecture health checks
  (ArchOps).

See the [roadmap](../roadmap/README.md).

## Technology scope

**Power BI is the entry point and the current focus.** The Microsoft ecosystem
(PBIP, Fabric, semantic models) is where the wedge is sharpest right now.

The Reflection *method* — metadata in → topology → narrative → baseline →
roadmap → enablement — is technology-agnostic and generalises to other semantic
and BI stacks over time. We deliberately do **not** chase that breadth yet.

The addressable market in Power BI alone, inside a single large agency, is more
than enough to build the company on. Focus beats breadth.

---

## Why this works for a government agency

- It is **non-threatening**: it opens by honouring what they built, not
  cataloguing what's wrong.
- It is **sovereign and safe**: nothing sensitive moves, and it can start on
  synthetic data.
- It is **defensible internally**: a tangible map, a baseline number, a
  prioritised roadmap — artefacts an official can show upward.
- It **lifts capability**: the team is more capable after you leave, with the
  tool in their hands.
