# Product Ladder — Method → Reflection → Engine

Three tiers. Only the top two are paid. A customer can enter at any rung, but
the natural path is to climb.

See the [product thesis](product-thesis.md) for the open-vs-paid philosophy.

---

## Tier 1 — The Method (open, free)

**What it is:** the published way of thinking — concepts, vocabulary, skills.

**What it costs:** nothing. It is open and transparent.

**Why it exists:** category-building, trust, demand generation. It teaches the
market to *see* analytics entropy, which makes them want the engine that
reduces it.

**Where it lives:** `/docs`, `/skills`, the public site.

---

## Tier 2 — The Reflection (paid, one-time front door)

**What it is:** the fixed-scope sovereign diagnostic and capability uplift. See
[the product definition](the-reflection.md) and the
[delivery workflow](../services/discovery-workflow.md).

**What it costs:** fixed price, ~2–4 weeks.

**Why it exists:** it is how a customer *tastes* the engine on their own estate,
at low risk, inside their own boundary. It produces the map, the baseline, the
opportunity map, the roadmap — and leaves the team more capable.

**The role it plays:** the smallest valuable thing you can sell. The front
door, not the destination.

---

## Tier 3 — The Engine (paid, recurring — the real product)

**What it is:** ongoing sovereign access to the continuously-improving engine
plus the acceleration cadence. This is the "development wheel" that keeps
turning.

**What it costs:** subscription / recurring, scoped to the estate.

**What it delivers:**

- a **living** reflection that re-runs as the estate evolves;
- continuously-sharpening detection (the compounding cross-estate intelligence);
- the [modular visual library](../concepts/modular-visual-intelligence.md) —
  reusable, branded, governed components the team assembles reports from;
- recurring architecture cadence (ArchOps): quarterly health checks, governance
  reviews, agent-assisted optimisation cycles.

**Why it exists:** this is what an agency buys when it wants to *accelerate*
rather than *audit-once*. It is the compounding product.

---

## How a customer climbs

```text
The Method        →   The Reflection      →   The Engine
(read, learn,         (one fixed-scope         (ongoing, living,
 adopt the mindset)    sovereign diagnostic)    compounding capability)

free, open            paid, one-time           paid, recurring
trust + demand        proof on their estate    the development wheel
```

The Method makes them want it. The Reflection proves it. The Engine sustains
and accelerates it.
