# Architecture Reflection

## Definition

Architecture reflection is the process of visualising and explaining an enterprise analytics ecosystem before attempting optimisation or governance analysis.

The purpose of reflection is:

- trust building
- shared understanding
- contextual awareness
- architectural visibility

---

## Core Principle

The platform must first demonstrate understanding.

Not criticism.

---

## Why This Matters

Enterprise analytics ecosystems are:

- evolved systems
- organisational artefacts
- collaborative outputs

Teams are often proud of what they built.

Immediate optimisation recommendations without contextual understanding reduce trust.

---

## Reflection Before Optimisation

The platform should first:

- visualise the ecosystem
- explain semantic relationships
- map lineage
- identify business domains
- surface reuse patterns

Only after reflection should:

- governance analysis
- entropy scoring
- optimisation
- remediation

be introduced.

---

## Initial Discovery Outputs

### Semantic Architecture Map

Visualise:

- reports
- pages
- datasets
- semantic models
- refresh chains
- gateways

---

### Consumption Patterns

Show:

- heavily reused assets
- semantic anchors
- critical business reports
- operational hotspots

---

### Lineage Reflection

Explain:

- how data flows
- where semantic reuse occurs
- where transformations accumulate

---

## Strategic Outcome

Reflection creates:

- psychological trust
- architectural visibility
- collaborative engagement

This establishes the foundation for semantic optimisation.
